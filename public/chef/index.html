Chef Compliance : regulatory compliance for enterprises

deployed
Problem Statement

Leveraging the underlying open source technology InSpec, enterprises needed a way to manage large compliance reports (datasets) and communicate control implementation and stature across their organization.

hypothesis and solution

Develop a 508 compliant application that allows enterprises to continously monitor their compliance stature, take action and implement controls across their entire hybrid infrastructure.

key responsibilities

User research, usability testing in EA and Beta, feature definition, big data visualization, visual design, web component development and UX.

Process and discovery

The process of developing the end-to-end UX for the Chef Compliance app required me to learn about the day to day tasks of our users. Our users consisted of system administrators and security engineers who spent a significant time on the commandline. It was important to understand the deficiancies of using the terminal in their workflow but also offer feature parody between the terminal and the web UI. I spent countless hours talking with our enterprise users in order to understand their pain points, business needs from managers and requirements from an infrastructure maturity perspective - which were then storymapped into an end-to-end story including installation, RBAC, user management, collaboration and asset management, and compliance failure correlation.
